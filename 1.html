Anatomy

Ganglia

Ganglia are cerebral centres of the sympathetic nervous system. There are seven principal ganglia of the nervous system:

  1. Sacral
  2. Prostatic
  3. Epigastric
  4. Cardiac
  5. Pharyngeal
  6. Post-nasal
  7. Pineal

The awakening of the ganglia bears witness to Nature, giving a knowledge of Life's Mistery. They reveal those higher states of consciousness in which one can apprehend the Cosmic Plan and consciously participate in it. Engergised ganglia may be described as concave rotating disks.

Pineal Gland

The pineal gland is the seat of the highest consciousness in man, his omniscent all-embracing mind. Although a seemingly useless appendage, when regenerated it carries the superphysical vision of the Ego to the highest planes of perception. In the aura of the pinal gland, during (the process of) thought, constant vibrations of light take place.
