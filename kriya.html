Preface

Kriya Yoga is a method for dissolving your false identity and attaining the recognition of being one with God.

Don't Mistake the Technique for the Goal
Techniques are the means, not the end goal. One must understand that techniques are used to reach a stage of realization, and once that stage is reached, the technique itself must be let go. When attachment to techniques becomes too strong, it can bind and prevent further growth. Remember, Kriya Yoga is a means, not the goal. God is the goal. Your true Self is the goal. Avoid becoming overly attached to the techniques themselves.

“You go often into the silence, but have you developed anubhava [‘direct experience’ of God]?” He was reminding me to love God more than meditation. “Do not mistake the technique for the Goal.”
– Bhaduri Mahasaya, The Levitating Saint, talking with Paramahansa Yogananda.

The Inner Guru
More important than any external Guru is the Inner Guru—God within you, the pure formless Consciousness. This Guru is not a chakra, a deity, or any specific form. When the emptiness of forms is realized, it becomes clear that it is Consciousness behind forms that is God, the true Guru. This SatGuru will guide you back to your true Self. Trust and surrender to this consciousness within, for it is your true Guru. Devotion must be directed toward your own consciousness, not a distant entity. Learn to love this true Guru within.

Kriyā Techniques

Kriyā Yoga is simple, but extremely effective.

Purpose: Our goal is to still the mind and Prāṇa in the brain region. Once the mind is under control, we just have to make it go through Amrita Nadi to the Spiritual Heart. This will not be done in a conventional manner.

There, the mind will dissolve, and the more often you take it there, the more you will start shifting from the ego-self to the True Self, until all there is, is Pure Bliss.

We will do the most simple Pranayama that gets the job of stilling the mind done, so we can easily turn it toward itself. This Pranayama is the most natural one. The more natural things are, the easier it is to control the mind.

“Everything is achievable with First Kriya. Parāvasthā and Divine intoxication, everything is there in First Kriya.”
– Lahiri Mahasaya, Garland of Letters

Later on, we will discover that it is because of the Parāvasthā state (After-Effect-State of Kriya) that everything can be accomplished with the First Kriya. It is not any particular Kriya technique, in and of itself, that will make you enlightened, but exclusively by abiding in the state of Parāvasthā.

Kriya Pranayama

The Kriya Pranayama we will be doing is very simple yet very effective at bringing the whole prana up to the brain region and stilling it there, quieting the mind. You can reach breathlessness with this Pranayama. It is so sweet and effortless; it is a pleasure to do. Although other Pranayamas are nice as well, they can be quite effortful and forceful.

Note: Always breathe through your nose. Mouth breathing can be detrimental to health.

Kriya Pranayama Instructions:

  1. Sit cross-legged in your normal position. No need for Padmasana or Siddhasana. Just an easy, relaxing cross-legged position.
  2. Pay attention to the point between your eyebrows (Third-Eye). Always keep your attention there during this practice. Don’t move 1 cm away from the Third-Eye.
  3. Inhale naturally while mentally chanting Om six times in the Third-Eye.
  4. Exhale naturally while mentally chanting Om six times in the Third-Eye.
  5. Your breathing doesn’t need to be with force or effort. Just natural breathing.
  6. Do this 108 times (1 full mala).

Eventually, all the prana will gather into the Sushumna Nadi, then move up to the Third-Eye and completely still the mind in joy. Never move your focus away from the Third-Eye. Always chant the 6+6 Oms in the point between your eyebrows. The breath will become subtler and longer. Ignore all thoughts and all chakras besides the Third-Eye. Just keep your focus on it until your whole consciousness is there.

Kriya Supreme Fire

Note: Make sure you can hold your breath for at least 90 seconds before attempting this practice.

Rules:

  • Avoid any pressure building up in the body; be gentle and not hurried.
  • Always keep your spine straight, and never allow your body to bend.
  • Don’t practice Kriya Supreme Fire with food in your stomach, as this practice will actively awaken the huge fire in your belly region.

At the beginning of this practice, you might feel a lot of heat, then you might start shaking or having some sort of mini-convulsions, and finally, a massive empty silence will fill you up.

Serious Warning: There might be side effects from this practice, like rage, furious attacks, impatience in daily life, pain in the body, itching in the legs, headaches, insomnia, etc. However, there can also be an increase in appetite, and a boost in self-confidence, willpower, and vital energy.

Above all, you might finally be able to clearly feel the Prāṇic energetic currents (Kuṇḍalinī). The biggest adverse symptom is aggressiveness. If you get too angry and things aren’t going well, just stop the practice for a while and reattempt again after some days.

Doing Pranayama afterward, or even beforehand, will help you to lessen the side effects. These two practices combine perfectly with Kriya Supreme Fire. (The sheer amount of energy that builds up with this practice is immense.)

Kriya Supreme Fire Instructions:

  1. Do Khechari Mudra, or put the tongue upward as much as you can.
  2. Focus on the Navel Zone, 3-4 fingerbreadths below the navel, or on the Perineum. Choose where it feels empty and spacious.
  3. Inhale up to 75-90% of full capacity, pulling the energy up from the Root Chakra toward the Heart Chakra.
  4. Hold the breath.
  5. Pull your lower abdomen in and up, and lock it strongly as in Uddiyana Bandha (Abdominal Lock), along with Mula Bandha (Perineum Lock) and Jalandhara Bandha (Neck Lock) as well. All 3 locks should be active.
  6. Relax and hold it for about 90 seconds, if possible in the Heart area instead of the belly area (you can build up to a maximum of 2 minutes, don’t do more than 2 minutes).
  7. You will feel a massive heat and will probably start shaking, but then… silence comes. Stay with that silence as long as you can, but don’t exceed the prescribed 2 minutes!
  8. Release and exhale. Notice how the Heart Chakra opens and joy is felt afterward.
  9. Relax and calm down.

Start by doing 1 round for two weeks, then attempt to do 2 rounds for another two weeks. If all is good, you can do 3 rounds. That is the maximum you should do until you are very confident with your practice. In that case, you can do as many as you are comfortable with.

This will burn lots of energetic blockages, but beware if it is too strong. Proceed with caution. The effects can be very powerful.

Routine

The sequence should be:

  • Kriya Supreme Fire
  • Kriya Pranayama
  • Rest (in Parāvasthā)

Alternatively, you can experiment with practicing Kriya Supreme Fire at the end of the routine. See which one brings you to the Parāvasthā state faster and stronger, and stick with it.

First Kriya – Final Words

The purpose of the First Kriya is simply to gather all the Prāṇa into the Sushumna Nadi and then upward into the brain area, into the Void there. Then the mind will be absolutely still and you can proceed to the Parāvastha state.

Parāvasthā

The most important part of Kriya Yoga is the Parāvasthā state. This is where the magic happens.

“If you want to know everything, then remain in the peace after performing Kriya.”
– Lahiri Mahasaya, Spiritual Commentaries on Srimad Bhagavad Gita

You need no external Guru. Your Consciousness is your Guru. Abiding in your blissful Consciousness exclusively (Parāvasthā) will make the mind lose its tendencies and desires, which in turn will make the attraction force of Maya fade, while the attraction Force of God will shine through. Once you are caught in the grip of Grace, you can't escape.

In the Final Kriya, instead of doing complicated techniques that are usually taught in Kriya Yoga, we will do either the “Conscious Parāvasthā” practice or “The After-Kriya Witness” practice. These are simply other names for the Parāvasthā. They are merely Self-Attention states, but consciously maintained and deepened.

The “Conscious Parāvasthā” practice is the sensation of being, the feeling of existing. You know you exist, right? How do you know it? Abide there, in that knowingness.

This will shower bliss in your heart and you will just stay with that bliss. It will get bigger and bigger until it will eventually melt the mind into the Heart. This is the Parāvasthā state, blissful being. Just being in this tranquil Blissful Being is the practice. Just being aware of being, of existing, abiding in your natural Presence, being conscious of being present.

That state you get after Kriya—recognize it, be fully aware of it, and you will see that it is a state where you are simply blissfully aware. Of what? Of Yourself, nothing else! Now you just have to consciously abide in it after your Kriya session, for as long as you can. Do not let the mind persuade you out of it, saying that it is too simple. The mind knows that it stands no chance against such a joyful empty conscious state.

“One should do what comes easily and without strain. Do not disturb tranquility by doing lots of Kriyas (Pranayamas).”
– Lahiri Mahasaya, Garland of Letters

You just have to stay in this Parāvasthā State. In your Presence, your sense of beingness. It is usually felt as a space in the Heart, a blissful empty space. This practice is so simple, but it is all you have to do to dissolve your false “I-ego.”

“One tall rule is: In Tranquility, do not do any Kriya.”
– Lahiri Mahasaya
