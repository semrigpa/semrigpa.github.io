
	Thought Power

	Thought Power or "Kriyashakti" from Sanskrit kriya 'action' and 
	shakti 'power', meaning "the power to do or create", is the 
	power of thought, and the key to the Siddhis of the the full Yogi.

	It is the mysterious and divine power latent in the will of 
	every human being, but is not strong enough to produce external 
	phenomena and remains dormant and becomes atrophied, unless 
	awakened and developed by yogic training.

		
	The Mental Body	

	The Thought/Mental Body or "Mayavi Rupa" from Sanskrit mayavi 
	'illusory' and rupa 'form, body', is a type of subtle body 
	created by the power of thought (Kriyashakti)/artificial vehicle of 
	consciousness. Due to being made of/It is made of subtle matter 
	and cannot be affected by material objects.

	The Thought Body must not be confused with the Astral Body, for 
	while the astral form is a real entity/being, the Thought Body 
	is a temporary illusion created by a/the mind. It is composed of 
	the whole principle of Kama, a portion of Manas and an emanation 
	from Buddhi.
	
	It can be created either consciously or unconsciously. An Adept 
	can use his Thought Body at will, but the average person cannot, 
	except in rare cases. It is called "Mayavi", because it is an 
	illusionary/illusory form created for use in a particular 
	instance. It contains enough of the Adept's mind to accomplish 
	its purpose.
