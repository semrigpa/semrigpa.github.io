Lesson 1 | Nouns

Notes

Nouns are words we can use to name things. First, we’ll see how to talk one thing (singular: shyin, 'dog') and more than one thing (plural: shyin-yé, 'the dogs'). Also, we’ll see how we can talk about something specific (shyin-la ‘the dog’), and something non-specific (in shyin ‘a dog’). Some languages classify nouns by gender. Nouns in Creole are though, nouns can be masculine or feminine.

Lesson 1 | Pronouns

Types of Pronoun

  • Subject – The subject is the one doing the action.

    Je manj diri. | I eat rice.

  • Object – The object is the one the action is done to.

    Yé linm mwa. | They like me.

  • Reflexive – If the subject and the object of a verb are the same, we need to use what’s called a reflexive pronoun like English ‘herself’, ‘myself’, etc.

    Li parl a li-minm. | She talks to herself.

  • Possesive – Possessive pronouns show who something belongs to.

  • Emphatic possessive – Emphatic possessive pronouns emphasize whose thing we’re talking about
