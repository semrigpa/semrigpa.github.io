Part 1 — The Evolution of Man

The Marriage of the Soul

As above, so below. Hermes Trismegistus

As in the universe so in man, as in the universal principle and finer bodies of man, so in the gross physical body.

When considering man, we cannot divorce the Divine Principle in man from man himself, and must regard the physical body as the vesture and manifestation of the God within.

So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them. Genesis 1:27

It is significant to recognised the fact that the human body is androgynous, and accurately speaking neither male nor female, but bisexual. In the male body the female organs of sex exist in a state of latent development; and in the female organism the male organs of sex are present in rudimentary form.

Upon the physical plane a dual force—male and female; positive and negative—is manifesting in every human being. The attraction between the sexes is the result of the effort found universally in Nature to balance these positive and negative forces.

In the day that God created man, in the likeness of God made he him; male and female created he them; and blessed them, and called their name Adam, in the day when they were created. Genesis 5:1-2

The Divine or Solar Force is both Father and Mother—positive and negative in its manifestation, yet at its Source maintained in a unity of sublime harmony and balance. This dual force is operative in man and its balance is found in the perfected man.

The ultimate goal of the individual soul's evolution on this planet is the formation of a deathless Solar Body—which can only evolve when a perfect balance of the positive and negative currents of Solar Force has been achieved. This is the marriage of the soul—the effort of the soul to balance self with its opposite.

Dual-Use of the Solar Force

The primordial elericity or Solar Force—semi-latent within the aura of every human being—is said to lie coiled up like a slumbering serpent.

It was known to the Greeks as the Speirêma (the serpent-coil), and in the Upanishads, the sacred writings of India, it is called Kundalinī (coiled-serpent).

Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the Lord God had made. And he said unto the woman, Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden? Genesis 3:1

In the Book of Genesis it is also symbolised as a Serpent. When this force stirred within Eve she was tempted to its misapplication.

When directed downward through the lower physical centres for generation, unhallowed by a consciousness of responsibility to God and the incoming soul, the Serpent Force or Fire brings knowledge of evil.

Directed upward toward the brain for regeneration, the formation of the deathless Solar Body, it brought knowledge of good. Hence the dual operation of the Solar Force is symbolised as the tree of the knowlege of good and evil.

And the Lord God said unto the serpent, Because thou hast done this, thou art cursed above all cattle, and above every beast of the field; upon thy belly shalt thou go, and dust shalt thou eat all the days of thy life: Genesis 3:14

The curse upon the serpent refers to the fact that for a period of human evolution man remains in ignorance of the Law governing the serpent (Solar Force), which manifests in man's lower earthly vehicles—misgoverned by the human mind.

And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel. Genesis 3:15

During this cycle of evolution, in man's ignorance of the Law governing the Serpent Fire, he continually directs it downward or bruise its head—while the Serpent Fire, thus misused, bruises man's heel, 'heel' being a symbol of the lowest earthly parts of man: the body, lower emotions and mortal mind.

And the Lord God said, Behold, the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil: and now, lest he put forth his hand, and take also of the tree of life, and eat, and live for ever: Genesis 3:22

The tree of life symbolises the upward play of the Solar Force for the creation of the deathless Solar body.

The force manifesting in generation is identical with that which, when rightly controlled, becomes the instrument of regeneration—the upbuilding of the Solar Body.

Men have called Love Eros, because he has wings, the Gods have called him Pteros, because he has the virtue of giving wings. Plato, 'The Banquet'

That which Nature binds, Nature also will dissolve—therefore, that which the soul binds, the soul will also dissolve. Nature bound the body to the soul, but the soul binds itself to the body. Nature liberates the body from the soul (in death), but the soul liberates itself from the body (in regeneration).

Part 2 — The Path to the Highest

Development of the Solar Body

The Human Constitution

The Philosophers hold that man is fourfold in nature, having four bodies that correspond to the four Elements.

  1. Fire – Spiritual/Divine or Solar Body
  2. Air – Mental Body
  3. Water – Astral Body
  4. Earth – Physical Body
  1. The physical or Earth body is interpenetrated by a body of finer matter vibrating at a higher rate, in which emotions and passions register, called the Water body.
  2. The Earth and Water bodies are interpenetrated by a body composed of still finer matter vibrating at a still higher rate, the mental body in which thoughts register, called the Air body.
  3. And informing these three bodies and engendering them is the Divine Spark, the potential Solar Body or God in man, existing as it were in embryo awaiting the evolution of the Earth, Water and Air bodies to sustain the flow of the Solar Force which shall stimulate and perfect its divine unfoldment.

One must learn to master the body, emotions and mind, and to differentiate appetites from emotions, and emotions from thoughts, for the purpose of gaining absolute control over the personal self. This mastery should be striven for, and may be achieved through concentration in meditation, and by persistent effort at all times to impress the mind to reject falsehood and accept only Truth that it may purely reflect the God within.

The Law of Immortality

The Law of Immorality

$$P \propto \frac{V}{D}$$

Permanence of consciousness is in direct proportion to the rate of vibration and in inverse proportion to the density of the medium.

The Solar Force or Life-giving Principle interpenetrates all matter and playing perpetually upon it causes different rates of vibration in its different densities. The more subtle the matter, the less the resistance to this force and the higher the vibration and resultant consciousness.

The higher the level of consciousness the more subtle the matter and the more refined the vibration. The evolution of consciousness is caused by the play of the life giving force or universal mind in matter.

The Order of The Philosophers

The Order of the Philosophers is is composed of those souls who have reached the crest of evolution on this planet, and who have passed beyond intellectualism into spiritual realisation.

They have renounced self (the personality). Renunciation is the word of power compelling admission to this Brotherhood of the Servants of God, and inevitably bringing association with its members through the attainment of that consciousness which transcends the barriers of time and space.

The aim of all souls who have attained to this level of consciousness is an entirely disinterested one--the stimulation of human evolution and the benefit of mankind.

Paracelsus

He who seeks divine knowledge will surely find it for the Divinity in man ever strives to render unto him his lost birthright.

Deep down in the human soul is implanted that divine instinct which reveals to man his oneness with God and his fellows.

Constant aspiration to know God's Law liberates in man that Force which is a Living Flame, and which acts under the direction of the God in man—with or without the conscious effort of the finite mind.

This Fire, once liberated, begins immediately to displace the sluggish nervous force and to open and perfect those nerve centres or minor brains, atrophied from disuse, and which when regenerated reveal to man superphysical states of consciousness and knowledge of his lost Sovereignty over Nature.

The Solar Force manifests on the physical plane by passing through the ganglia of the sympathetic nervous system and thence up the spine to the brain where its currents unite to build up the deathless Solar or Spiritual Body.

Knowledge as to the development of this Force has been guarded in all ages lest man, through ignorance, should employ it to his destruction.

That soul who will renounce all personal ambition, and will seek by selfless service of his fellow beings to obey the Divine Spirit within may, without external teaching or assistance, evoke this Flame and achieve unaided a knowledge of Nature's secrets and mysteries. But unless governed by the God within, and with selfless purpose this Fire will intensify the lower passions and make the man a destructive force working contrary to the Law of Nature.

The Stone of the Philosophers

The Philosopher's Stone is a term used by Alchemists to denote the Supreme Wisdom, the union of the divine consciousness in man with the lower consciousness, which union has been the goal of Initiates in all ages. Symbolically, it is the secret of the transmutation of base metals into gold.

It is the key to that divine Knowledge which is the same in all ages, and superior to all faiths and philosophies—that secret science which is in reality secret only because it is hidden and locked in the inner nature of every man, however ignorant and humble, and none but himself can turn the key.

The Supreme Wisdom is known when realized by an acute intellect—purified by meditation and self-control. By a knowledge of the Truth man assuredly attains bliss even in the flesh.
